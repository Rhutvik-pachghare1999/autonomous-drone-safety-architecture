The Problem Statement (The "Feasibility Gap")
Problem: The integration of Vision-Language-Action (VLA) foundation models into robotics provides immense semantic reasoning capabilities, but introduces a fatal "Feasibility Gap." Foundation models are fundamentally non-deterministic and prone to hallucinations; they frequently generate commands (e.g., "fly directly into the ground to inspect the dirt") that violate the kinodynamic boundaries of the physical drone. Current edge-compute architectures either rely on slow middleware (ROS2/Python) that fails to catch these errors in real-time, or use simplistic safety filters (1st-order CBFs) that cause mathematical instability when applied to the 2nd-order dynamics of a quadrotor.

The Objectives
To solve this, your Master's research will execute the following four objectives:

Mathematical Safety via HOCBF: Mathematically derive and implement a relative-degree-2 High-Order Control Barrier Function (HOCBF) that dynamically maps non-deterministic semantic velocity commands into strictly bounded, physically safe thrust and attitude limits.
Deterministic Edge Latency ($<100\mu s$ WCET): Prove that safety filters can operate with strict hard real-time deadlines on constrained edge hardware (Raspberry Pi 5) by utilizing PREEMPT_RT CPU core isolation, memory locking (mlockall), and atomic mmap zero-copy shared memory, entirely bypassing Python/ROS serialization overhead.
Zero-Shot Sim-to-Real via Asymmetric RL: Replace brittle PID controllers with a robust Reinforcement Learning (RL) policy trained via Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO) in Isaac Sim under massive domain randomization (e.g., $\pm 30%$ mass variation), proving the safety kernel holds even when the physical plant degrades.
Hardware-in-the-Loop (HIL) Hallucination Stress Testing: Empirically validate the architecture by intentionally feeding adversarial prompts to a PaliGemma-3B VLA model, demonstrating 100% collision-free survival rates when the AI generates hostile flight paths.
